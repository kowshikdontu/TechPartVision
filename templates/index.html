About our Vision

Power of Deep Learning

We have built a sequential-based convolutional neural network (CNN) using TensorFlow and Keras. The Sequential model is ideal for building simple and straightforward neural network architectures. Our model consists of multiple convolutional layers interspersed with batch normalization and activation layers, followed by max-pooling layers to reduce the spatial dimensions of the data. This is capped with a dense layer for the final classification. By integrating data augmentation techniques, our model is designed to enhance the robustness and accuracy of the classification task for computer hardware parts.

Transfer Learning

We utilized transfer learning to enhance the accuracy and efficiency of our image classification model. By leveraging a pre-trained convolutional neural network (such as VGG16, ResNet, or Inception) and fine-tuning it on our specific dataset of computer hardware parts, we capitalized on the robust feature extraction capabilities of the pre-trained model. This allowed us to achieve high performance with fewer training epochs and reduced computational resources.

Components Categorization

Our model was trained to categorize images of the following 14 computer hardware components:

  • Webcam
  • Cables
  • Case
  • CPU
  • GPU
  • HDD
  • Headset
  • Keyboard
  • Microphone
  • Monitor
  • Motherboard
  • Mouse
  • RAM
  • Speakers
